Gstaad

Price starting from 490,00 €

Gstaad is a bilingual village in the German-speaking district of the Canton of Bern in southwestern Switzerland. It is part of the municipality of Saanen and is known as the largest ski resort and popular destination among high society and international elite. The winter campus of Institute Le Rosey is located in Gstaad. Gstaad has a population of approximately 3,200 and is located 1,050 meters (3,445 feet) above sea level. Tourism in Gstaad began in 1577 when the first hotel was built there. The largest of the currently operating hotels "Gstaad Palace" opened in 1913.

St. Moritz

Price starting from 1490,00 €

St. Moritz is a resort town in the Engadine valley in Switzerland. It is a municipality in the Maloja district in the Swiss canton of Graubünden. The highest summit in the Eastern Alps, Piz Bernina, is located several kilometers south of the city. St. Moritz hosted the Winter Olympics in 1928 and 1948. It is a popular destination for high society and international elite, as well as one of the most expensive ski resorts in the world. St. Moritz is also considered the birthplace of bobsledding. Major bobsled, luge and skeleton competitions are held on the track in St. Moritz.

Méribel

Price starting from 590,00 €

Méribel is a ski resort in the Tarentaise Valley in the French Alps, located near the town of Moutiers and the Vanoise National Park. It is located at an altitude of 1400 m above sea level. The founder of the resort is Peter Lindsay, who was looking for a place for ski slopes that would not be in Austria or Germany due to the growing Nazi movement there. He first visited these places in 1936, and in 1938 the first lift was built. During the war, construction was suspended, but three years after the end of the war, construction resumed. During the Olympic Games in Albertville, some competitions were held here.

Avoriaz

Price starting from 390,00 €

Avoriaz (French: Avoriaz, pronounced and sometimes written as Avoria) is a ski resort in France, one of the 12 stations of the famous Portes du Soleil ski area. It is located in the Chablais geographical region, between Mont Blanc and Lake Geneva. The resort consists of 5 villages: De la Falaise, De Crozats, De Rousses, De Dromont, De Haut Fort, Du Festival. From 1973 to 1993, a film festival was held here. Unlike other French ski resorts that have a rich "non-skiing" history, Avoriaz was built immediately as a resort, right on a deserted plateau that once served local shepherds for grazing livestock. The first skiers came to the newly built Avoriaz in 1966.

Chamonix

Price starting from 390,00 €

Chamonix-Mont-Blanc, or simply Chamonix, is a city and commune in eastern France, in the Haute-Savoie department. Population - 9,830 inhabitants (1999). It is located in the Alps, in the narrow valley of the Arve River (a tributary of the Rhône), at the foot of Mont Blanc, where a tunnel has been dug at Chamonix connecting France with Italy. Chamonix is the center of the popular Chamonix-Mont-Blanc ski region. The Chamonix Valley was first mentioned in 1091. In 1924, the city gained wide fame thanks to the hosting of the I Winter Olympic Games here. By the middle of the 20th century, agriculture, which was once an important source of income for local residents, lost its popularity and economic activity in the city finally moved to the tourism sector.

Courchevel

Price starting from 549,00 €

Courchevel is the name of a ski resort located in the commune of Saint-Bon-Tarentaise in the French Alps, in the Tarentaise Valley, Savoie, Rhône-Alpes region, France. It is part of Les Trois Vallées, the largest connected ski area in the world. Courchevel also refers to the towns of Courchevel 1300 (Le Praz), Courchevel 1550, Courchevel 1650 (Moriond) and Courchevel 1850, which are named after their heights in meters. They are located in the commune of Saint-Bon-Tarentaise. The center of the Courchevel resort is actually at 1,747 meters (5,732 feet). The somewhat misleading name Courchevel 1850 was chosen for marketing reasons to compete with the competitor Val d'Isère. It is the Jardin Alpin area of Courchevel 1850 (not the center) that is actually located at 1,850 meters (6,070 feet).

Davos

Price starting from 1390,00 €

Davos is a municipality in the Prättigau/Davos district in the canton of Graubünden, Switzerland. It has a permanent population of 11,211 (2013). Davos is located on the Landwasser River, in the Swiss Alps, between the Plessur and Albula ranges. At 1,560 m (5,120 ft) it is the highest city in Europe. Davos is the host of the World Economic Forum, an annual gathering of global political and business elites and home to one of Switzerland's largest ski resorts. At the end of each year, it serves as the venue for the annual Spengler Cup ice hockey tournament, organized by the local Davos hockey team HC.

Megève

Price starting from 390,00 €

Megève is known as one of the popular ski resorts in Europe. The tourism industry here began to develop in 1913, when the Office du Tourisme (French: Office du Tourisme - tourist information bureau) opened in Megève. However, the real boom began after Baron Maurice de Rothschild and Baroness Noémie de Rothschild, who wanted to make a French station a worthy competitor to the most prestigious Swiss resort of St. Moritz, built the first luxury hotel in this village in the Savoy Alps in 1921. Since the 1950s, Megève has become a holiday destination for celebrities who are fond of winter sports.
